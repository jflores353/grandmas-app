Merejilda

A life full of Love

Eulogy

Merejilda was a compassionate and trustworthy lady, with glamour’s smiles and sparkling brown eyes. Her affectionate and charismatic personality revealed a person of peace and love, who desired harmony as a way of life. She was inspirational and nurturing and taught her loved ones how to handle a blessing or adversity while bringing out the best in them. She enjoyed a close relationship with those she loved and was significantly parental in nature. She was always one to show affection be it with a simple handshake, a hug, proper greeting, or a contagious smile. For when you do one of the aforementioned jesters you get one in return. She taught her children to respect, manners, integrity and showed them to mature being independent. Merejilda was an active and enthusiastic woman who pursued life with gusto and lived life to the fullest. She made a sufficient impact on family, friends, and people she met, and never said no to offer a helping hand. She leaves her family and friends with a treasury of memories and life lessons. Merejilda had a strong faith and demonstrated that GOD was important in her daily life. She was a member of the St Joseph Catholic Church. Merejilda and Roman served the Lord together in the joys and sorrows of life. She was a devoted spouse, mother, grandmother, sister, and friend.

Merejilda’s story began in 1924. She was born in Lockhart, Texas to Juanita and Mercedes Rosales on April 2nd. She was their last born. She was joined by her brothers Martin, Fidel, Mauricio, Rene, and her sister Dominga. At the age of 1 ½, Merejilda and her siblings were adopted by Josefa Ramos and Juan Gonzales, due to a family tragedy. Therefore, the six siblings were all raised in the San Marcos, Texas area. As adults, her siblings reunited to the Lubbock, Texas area and were buried in the vicinity upon their death. While living with her new mom she would often provide assistance with her mom’s profession. Josefa was a seamstress, midwife, and herbalist who practiced unlicensed health and medical care to the Hispanic community as early as the 1900s. Josefa did this work due to the Jim Crow Laws of racism which started in 1890 and continued until 1965. Merejilda provided minor medical care or advice to many Hispanics and family members who sought her services due to medical costs. She married Roman Garcia Flores on August 17, 1942, in Kingsville, Texas. Merejilda was 18 and Roman was 25. Merejilda and Roman were married for seventy-five years. This is the most joyful gift that few will encounter in their lifetime.

Merejilda did various tasks during her work career. Her main job was taking care of the household, as she took responsibility for care of her family. She also raised and cared for many of her grandchildren and children of her friends. She loved the outdoors and worked in her garden and yard. She was well known for her cooking skills, appetizing and delicious dishes. Family members, relatives, friends, and sometimes strangers left her house with satisfying and content smiles due to a full stomach and enjoyable conversations. She loved family gathering and seeing her family talking, playing and enjoying each other in peace. She was known for her hospitable nature, caring demeanor, and her home was always filled with people and activity. But now she is free, there will be no more struggles, all is left behind. She has found peace with the Lord.

Merejilda was preceded in death by both her biological and adopted parents. She again grieved with the passing of all her siblings, numerous relatives and friends throughout her life. Her new family mourned with the loss of her first child Anna on January 4, 2008, and her second child Carmelite on December 21, 2012, her third child Paula on June 23, 2016, and the man of her life Roman Flores who passed on September 30, 2017, as a Centurion.

Merejilda and Roman raised two nephews Edward and Philip due to the loss of their parents and that started a family of great joy and happiness. Then the Lord blessed Merejilda and Roman with 4 girls and 7 boys during their marriage, in the following order, Anna, Carmelita, Benny, Paula, Francis, Joe, Roman (Jr.), Ricky, Johnny, Marty, and Steven.

Merejilda is symbolized by the dove of peace. She was an inspirational role model. She valued order and cherished tradition, and the purity of home, and family. She established important steps about growing up to become good citizens. She was a shining example for showing love, faith, integrity, manners, respect, responsibility, happiness and right from wrong. She taught her family not to be prejudiced or judgmental. Her family is a better group of adults because of her life experiences, words of wisdom, and great influence in their lives. Her kindness, love, laughter, “and most of all Roman “will never be forgotten, but he will be missed. She grieved at the loss of so many lives during her numerous years of life. Those who knew Merejilda Flores know how she spent her days on earth. Those who have left before her will welcome her with smiles and open arms.
